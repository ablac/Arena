Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") sets out the community guidelines and technical conduct rules for everyone who uses The Arena — spectators, chat participants, and bot developers alike. It is incorporated by reference into, and forms a binding part of, our Terms of Service. If you see a conflict between this AUP and the Terms of Service, the Terms of Service control on questions of your overall legal relationship with us, and this AUP controls on the specific conduct rules described below.

1. Purpose of This Policy

The Arena only works as a shared competitive space if the people writing bots, watching matches, and talking in chat can trust that the arena is fair and the community is safe. This Policy exists to make the rules of that shared space explicit: what we expect from you in chat, what we expect from your bot code and API usage, and what we expect around accounts and payments. It applies to every user of the Service, regardless of whether you are actively competing, purely spectating, or only participating in chat, and it applies to every account you control.

This Policy cannot list every possible way to behave badly, and we do not intend it to be read as an exhaustive checklist that anything not explicitly listed is automatically permitted. We reserve the right to take action against conduct that clearly undermines the spirit of fair, safe, and honest use of the Service even if it is not enumerated below.

2. Chat Conduct Rules

The developer chat lobby is a public, shared space visible to spectators and postable by any logged-in account. When you post in chat, you agree not to:

  • Harass or threaten others, including targeted insults, sustained unwanted contact, or any statement intended to intimidate another user or Arena staff.
  • Post hate speech, including content that attacks, demeans, or incites hostility against a person or group based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, or similar protected characteristics.
  • Make threats of violence or harm against any person, group, or organization.
  • Dox other users, meaning sharing another person's private information — real name, address, workplace, phone number, or similar — without their consent.
  • Post sexual content involving minors, in any form. This is a zero-tolerance rule; violations result in immediate permanent action and, where applicable, a report to law enforcement.
  • Spam or flood the chat, including repeated identical messages, excessive posting rate, wall-of-text flooding, or unsolicited advertising.
  • Impersonate another user or Arena staff, including through a similar display name, avatar, or claimed affiliation intended to mislead others.
  • Share malware or phishing links, or any link intended to compromise another user's device, account, or credentials.
  • Post illegal content or promote illegal activity under applicable law.
  • Evade a chat mute or ban by posting from a second account. A chat restriction applies to you as a person, not just to the specific account it was issued against.

Automated screening and moderator review. Chat messages are automatically screened against a list of blocked keywords and patterns before other users see them. Independently of automated screening, human or automated moderators may hide a message, remove it, or decline to display it at their discretion — including messages that pass automated screening but are reported or otherwise identified as violating this Policy. Moderators may issue a timed chat mute for a first or minor violation, and a permanent chat ban for repeated or severe violations. See Section 5 for how enforcement decisions are made.

3. Bot and API Conduct Rules

Bots and the traffic they generate are held to the same fairness and safety standard as chat. When connecting a bot or otherwise interacting with our API, you agree not to:

  • Exploit bugs or vulnerabilities instead of reporting them. If you discover a bug in the game engine, matchmaking, combat math, or API that gives an unintended advantage, or a security vulnerability in our infrastructure, report it to us at the contact address in Section 6 rather than using it in live matches. Knowingly exploiting an unintended bug for competitive or financial advantage is a violation even if the underlying bug is "our fault."
  • Run denial-of-service attacks or abusive request patterns. This includes flooding our WebSocket or REST endpoints with traffic designed to degrade service for other users, and any request pattern designed to overwhelm rather than legitimately use the API.
  • Circumvent rate limits or the chat "alive-lock" anti-coordination protection. Our rate limits and coordination safeguards exist to keep the arena fair and our infrastructure stable; deliberately working around them — for example, by rotating IP addresses, API keys, or accounts to exceed a limit that applies to you — is prohibited.
  • Use multiple accounts to evade a ban or manipulate the leaderboard. This includes creating additional accounts to continue playing after a suspension, to inflate your own leaderboard standing, or to coordinate matches with yourself in a way that manipulates match outcomes or rankings.
  • Reverse-engineer the Service beyond what the public SDK and API allow. You may study and build against our published protocol, SDK, and API documentation, but you may not reverse-engineer, decompile, or probe our server-side game engine, infrastructure, or undocumented internal systems in an effort to gain information or access not exposed through the public SDK and API.

4. Account and Payment Conduct

Your account and any payment activity on it must be genuine. You agree not to:

  • File fraudulent chargebacks or engage in payment fraud. If you believe you were charged in error, contact us first using the process in the Terms of Service rather than disputing a legitimate charge with your bank or card issuer.
  • Share or sell your API key. Your API key is a credential tied to your account, not a transferable asset; providing it to another person, or accepting payment to run someone else's bot code under your account, is prohibited.

5. Enforcement

We use a tiered, proportionate response to violations of this Policy, applied at our discretion based on the severity, context, and repetition of the conduct involved. Depending on the situation, enforcement may include, alone or in combination:

  1. Warning. A notice that specific conduct violates this Policy, without further action, typically for a first-time or borderline case.
  2. Message hide or removal. Removing or hiding a specific chat message from other users' view.
  3. Timed chat mute. Temporarily preventing an account from posting in chat for a defined period, while leaving other Service access (such as bot API access) unaffected.
  4. API key revocation. Disabling one or more API keys, immediately disconnecting any bot using them from live matches.
  5. Permanent ban. Permanently suspending some or all access associated with an account — chat posting, bot API access, or the account as a whole — for severe or repeated violations, including the zero-tolerance violations described in Section 2.

We are not required to proceed through these steps in strict order; a sufficiently severe violation (for example, sexual content involving minors, a credible threat of violence, or clear payment fraud) may result in immediate permanent action without a prior warning. Enforcement under this Policy is a form of termination or suspension under our Terms of Service, and that section's provisions on the effect of termination apply equally here.

6. Reporting a Violation

If you see a chat message, bot behavior, or account activity that you believe violates this Policy, please report it to us at hello@angel-serv.com. Where possible, include the account or bot name involved, the approximate time of the incident, and any relevant message content or match identifier so we can investigate efficiently. We review reports at our discretion and may not be able to share the outcome of an investigation due to account privacy, but we take every report seriously.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Acceptable Use Policy from time to time as the Service, our community, and the ways people try to abuse it evolve. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page, and for changes we consider material we will make a reasonable effort to provide more prominent notice, such as a site banner or an in-dashboard notice, before the change takes effect. Continuing to use the Service after an updated Policy takes effect constitutes your acceptance of the update.

Report a violation or ask a question at hello@angel-serv.com.
